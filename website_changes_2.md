## Patterns That Work (Pulled from All Sites)

### Copy Rules

1. **One headline, max two lines.** Not three. Not a paragraph.
2. **Lead with the problem or the outcome, never the method.** "Wrestling with projects?" not "Project management software."
3. **One CTA.** Not three buttons competing. One clear next step.
4. **No feature lists in the hero.** Save that for below the fold.
5. **Use "you" before "we."** The visitor's problem comes first.

### Visual Rules

1. **Animation/visual on the right or behind text - never competing with it.**
2. **Dark or bold backgrounds make text pop.** White backgrounds need very strong typography.
3. **Typing/cycling effects work for showing range** - but they need a strong punchline to land on.
4. **Subtle > flashy.** The best animations feel like breathing, not fireworks.

### Structure That Converts

```
[Headline - 1-2 lines, problem-first or outcome-first]
[Subline - 1 line, positions GARIHC's unique value]
[Single CTA button - conversational, like "Let's talk"]
[Visual/animation - product demo, cycling text, or subtle motion]
```

## The GARIHC Hero Direction

Based on everything above, here's the framework locked in:

**Mechanic:** Cycling/typing text that shows what clients *think* they need, then reveals GARIHC's real value.

**Structure:**
```
Static:    "You think you need"
Cycling:   [a new website / an AI chatbot / a mobile app / better analytics]
Reveal:    [Single line that captures GARIHC's diagnostic superpower]
Subline:   [One sentence positioning]
CTA:       [Conversational - "Let's start with a conversation" or similar]
```

**The reveal line is the hardest part.** It needs to capture:
- "I see what you're not seeing"
- "I diagnose before I build"
- "I read your mind before you finish explaining"

Without sounding generic, arrogant, or like every other consultant.

**Visual element options:**
- Typing cursor animation (like Codecademy)
- Soft gradient shift behind text (like Cohere)
- Minimal product/work preview fading in (like Linear)

## What NOT to Do (Anti-Patterns)

- ❌ "Strategy · Technology · Taste" - tells a cold visitor nothing
- ❌ Three separate CTA buttons in the hero
- ❌ Feature lists or service breakdowns above the fold
- ❌ Stock images or generic AI visuals
- ❌ Explaining GARIHC as an acronym before saying what you do
- ❌ "Welcome to GARIHC" or any greeting-style headline
- ❌ Long paragraphs in the hero - if it's more than 20 words, it's too much
